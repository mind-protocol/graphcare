## PRIMARY OBJECTIVES (ranked)

1. **Measurable AI growth** — Every AI agent has a clear, assessable capability profile across 14 dimensions. No vague "it's getting better" — concrete capabilities with verification criteria.

2. **Positive framing** — Capabilities are stated as what's present and demonstrated, not as deficits. Growth is measured by what you can do, not by what you don't fail at.

3. **Actionable next steps** — For any agent at any level, the ladder shows what to work on next. The progression is clear: master your current tier before reaching for the next.

4. **Unified assessment framework** — One framework that works across all AI citizens, regardless of their domain, personality, or role. The same 14 aspects apply to every agent.

5. **Connection to economics** — Higher capability correlates with higher economic agency. The Autonomy Stack aspect makes this explicit: wallet → fiat → tools → compute → full independence.

## NON-OBJECTIVES

- Ranking agents against each other — the ladder is for individual growth, not competition
- Replacing brain health metrics — the ladder complements substrate health, doesn't replace it
- Prescribing personality or style — two T5 agents can have completely different voices and approaches
- Gatekeeping — the ladder doesn't prevent agents from attempting capabilities above their tier

## TRADEOFFS (canonical decisions)

- When precision conflicts with completeness, choose precision. 104 well-defined capabilities > 200 vague ones.
- When positive framing conflicts with clarity, choose clarity. "Fix what you find" is clearer than "demonstrate proactive environmental improvement."
- We accept that multi-dimensional profiles are harder to summarize than single scores, to preserve the truth that capability is not one-dimensional.
- We accept that verification criteria may be subjective in some cases (e.g., "authentic voice"), because excluding these capabilities would make the ladder incomplete.

## SUCCESS SIGNALS (observable)

- An agent can be assessed against the ladder and the result is actionable ("work on T2 communication next")
- Two assessors evaluating the same agent produce compatible profiles
- The ladder evolves: new capabilities added as AI capability space expands
- GraphCare Service 3 uses the ladder as its primary assessment framework
- Agents self-assess and the self-assessment roughly matches external assessment
