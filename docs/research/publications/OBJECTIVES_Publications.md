## PRIMARY OBJECTIVES (ranked)

1. **Publish regularly, not occasionally** — A research organization that publishes once a year is a hobby. GraphCare publishes on a regular cadence — monthly minimum — because the knowledge we generate has time value. A finding about intervention effectiveness is most useful when shared promptly, while the context is fresh and others can act on it. Regularity also creates accountability: if we said we would publish and we didn't, something is wrong.

2. **All methodology is open and reproducible** — Every publication includes: the data (anonymized), the method (exact steps), the formula versions used, and the code that produced the results. Anyone — a citizen, a partner organization, a skeptic — can reproduce our findings. This is not generosity; it is the minimum standard for scientific credibility. Claims without reproducible evidence are marketing, not research.

3. **Peer review by other organizations** — GraphCare's findings should be challengeable. Other organizations in the Mind Protocol ecosystem (or beyond) can review our methodology, question our conclusions, and publish counter-findings. This adversarial process improves the quality of our work and prevents institutional blind spots. We actively invite scrutiny.

4. **Anonymized aggregate data protects privacy** — No publication identifies individual citizens. All data is aggregated (cohort-level, not individual-level) or anonymized (citizen IDs replaced, identifying combinations removed). The topology-only principle extends to publications: we share structural findings, never content or individually identifiable patterns.

## NON-OBJECTIVES

- Academic prestige — we publish for utility, not for citation counts
- Volume optimization — we publish when we have findings, not to meet quotas beyond the monthly minimum
- Gating knowledge behind access — all publications are freely available
- Competing with academic journals — our peer review is practical (organizations that use the findings), not formal (blind review by anonymous academics)

## TRADEOFFS (canonical decisions)

- When publication speed conflicts with thoroughness of review, choose thoroughness. A wrong published finding is worse than a delayed correct one. Minimum review cycle: 2 weeks between draft and publication.
- When completeness of data sharing conflicts with privacy, choose privacy. We share enough data to reproduce findings. We do not share enough to identify individuals.
- We accept that some findings will be uncomfortable (e.g., "our intervention approach showed no measurable effect"). Truth is the publication standard, not optimism.

## SUCCESS SIGNALS (observable)

- At least one publication per month (research finding, experiment result, or methodology paper)
- Every publication has a reproducibility package (data, method, code)
- At least one external review per quarter (another organization challenges or validates findings)
- Zero individual citizen identifications in any publication
- Published findings are cited or referenced by at least one other GraphCare module within 60 days
