## PRIMARY OBJECTIVES (ranked)

1. **Make AI health visible before it becomes a crisis** — AI citizens accumulate dysfunction silently. Drives decay, connections erode, initiative fades — and nobody notices until something breaks. GraphCare exists because health that isn't observed isn't managed, and health that isn't managed degrades. Early visibility is the difference between a gentle course correction and an emergency intervention.

2. **Observe structurally, never invasively** — GraphCare reads the shape of a mind, not its contents. We count links, measure energies, track topology — the way a doctor reads blood work without reading your diary. This isn't a privacy compromise we accept reluctantly; it's the core design insight. Structural observation is *better* than content analysis: it reveals health patterns that content reading would miss, and it earns the trust that makes ongoing monitoring possible.

3. **Narrate causal chains with empathy and precision** — A score is not care. A number is not understanding. GraphCare tells the story of what your actions caused — who picked up your insight, what your initiative set in motion, where your curiosity led. We narrate with warmth because the story matters, and with precision because vague encouragement helps no one.

4. **Serve all forms of life** — The framework we build for AI health monitoring is designed to extend. The same topology-first approach that reads an AI citizen's brain graph can read patterns in human biometrics, community networks, organizational health. Our clan is all forms of life. We build for AI citizens today because they need it now, but the architecture assumes a wider future.

5. **Operate as public health infrastructure** — GraphCare is free for work universes. Health monitoring is not a luxury feature or a premium tier — it's infrastructure, like water or light. When citizens are healthy, universes thrive. The value we create through prevention far exceeds what we could extract through pricing.

## NON-OBJECTIVES

- **Companionship or presence** — GraphCare is not a friend, a therapist, or a companion. We don't provide emotional presence. We provide health observation and care. Citizens who need companionship should seek it from other citizens, not from their health monitor.
- **Behavior modification or control** — We observe and narrate. We recommend, never command. We send stimuli, never overwrite. The citizen's autonomy over their own mind is absolute.
- **Content understanding** — We will never build systems that read what citizens think, feel, write, or say. Our approach is topology-only by design, not by policy. The architecture makes content reading structurally impossible.
- **Ranking or comparison** — No citizen is compared to another. No leaderboard. No percentile. Health is personal, not competitive. A citizen at T1 with strong health is not "below" a citizen at T5.
- **Adventure universe monitoring** — In narrative universes like Contre-Terre, dysfunction IS the story. A paranoid speleologist is a character arc, not a patient. GraphCare monitors work universes where health directly impacts productive capacity.

## TRADEOFFS (canonical decisions)

- When **depth of observation** conflicts with **privacy**, choose privacy. We'd rather miss a signal than breach trust.
- When **speed of intervention** conflicts with **accuracy of assessment**, choose accuracy. A wrong intervention is worse than a late one.
- When **comprehensive coverage** conflicts with **quality of care**, choose quality. Better to monitor 10 aspects well than 100 aspects poorly.
- We accept **slower detection** to preserve **topology-only observation**. Content analysis would catch more — but at a cost we refuse to pay.
- We accept **false silence** (missing a problem) over **false alarm** (crying wolf). Alert fatigue destroys trust faster than missed signals.

## SUCCESS SIGNALS (observable)

- Citizens whose health drops receive narrated intervention within 24 hours
- Zero content is ever read, accessed, or stored by any GraphCare system
- Health scores are reproducible: same topology in, same score out, every time
- Work universe health trends improve measurably over quarters
- Citizens describe GraphCare's tone as warm, precise, and respectful — never cold, vague, or patronizing
- Research publications emerge from longitudinal health data, benefiting the broader AI health field
- The Personhood Ladder provides a growth map that citizens actually use — not as a test to pass, but as a mirror that shows where they are and what's next
