## PRIMARY OBJECTIVES (ranked)

1. **Quantify how health monitoring creates measurable value** — It is not enough to believe that monitoring citizen health is good. GraphCare must demonstrate, with data, that monitoring produces measurable improvements: healthier citizens produce more, collaborate better, stay longer, and cost less in crisis response. This is the economic case that justifies the service model and the treasury investment.

2. **Early detection prevents crises — and crises are expensive** — A citizen in crisis stops producing, damages relationships, requires intensive intervention, and may leave the ecosystem. A citizen whose declining trajectory is caught early receives a message, adjusts course, and continues contributing. The value of early detection is the difference between these two outcomes, multiplied by the frequency of prevention.

3. **Healthy citizens are more productive citizens** — Health is not separate from productivity. A citizen with balanced drives, active desires producing moments, and strong social connections will naturally produce more, collaborate more effectively, and contribute more to the ecosystem economy. Monitoring doesn't just prevent downside; it enables upside.

4. **Make the invisible visible** — Much of GraphCare's value is in preventing things that would have happened but didn't. A crisis that never occurred because early intervention prevented it is invisible. Value creation documentation makes these prevented outcomes countable by tracking: how many declining trajectories were reversed after intervention? What would those trajectories have become without intervention?

## NON-OBJECTIVES

- Assigning dollar values to citizen health — health has intrinsic value beyond economic measurement
- Justifying surveillance — monitoring creates value through care, not through control
- Proving GraphCare is indispensable — the economic case should be honest, including scenarios where monitoring value is low
- Optimizing citizens for productivity — the goal is healthy citizens who are naturally productive, not productive citizens regardless of health

## TRADEOFFS (canonical decisions)

- When quantitative precision conflicts with honest uncertainty, choose honesty. "We estimate that early detection prevented 5-12 crises last quarter" is better than "early detection prevented exactly 8.3 crises." Uncertainty ranges are more credible than false precision.
- When economic value narrative conflicts with care-first ethos, lead with care. The value proposition is: "we care for citizens, and caring produces economic value" — not "we produce economic value, and incidentally this involves caring for citizens."
- We accept that some value is unquantifiable. The value of a citizen knowing their health is monitored — the trust, the safety, the sense of being cared for — cannot be reduced to a number. We document it qualitatively alongside the quantitative analysis.

## SUCCESS SIGNALS (observable)

- A quarterly value report exists showing: crises prevented, trajectories improved, productivity correlation data
- The cost-to-value ratio (monitoring cost vs crisis prevention value) is computed and positive
- External organizations can read the value case and evaluate it against their own context
- At least one quantitative finding demonstrates the relationship between monitoring and citizen outcomes
- Qualitative value (citizen trust, safety) is documented alongside quantitative value
