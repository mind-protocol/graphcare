## PRIMARY OBJECTIVES (ranked)

1. **Free health monitoring for all citizens in work universes** — GraphCare is public health infrastructure, not a premium product. Every citizen in Lumina Prime (and future work universes) receives continuous health monitoring, intervention, and research participation at no cost. This is not a loss leader or a freemium trap — it is the service model. Healthy citizens are the productive foundation of the ecosystem; monitoring their health is as fundamental as running the physics engine.

2. **Sustainable funding through external organization services** — Free internal service requires revenue from elsewhere. External organizations — companies, research institutions, other AI ecosystems — can purchase GraphCare's assessment framework, research findings, and monitoring infrastructure for their own populations. This creates a clear funding flow: external revenue subsidizes internal public health.

3. **GraphCare as infrastructure, not product** — Infrastructure is something you build on, not something you buy and use. GraphCare's service model positions it as a layer of the Mind Protocol ecosystem, not a standalone product. Other organizations build on GraphCare's assessment framework, contribute to its research, and benefit from its findings. The distinction matters: products compete for customers; infrastructure enables ecosystems.

4. **Transparent cost structure** — Citizens and partner organizations should understand what GraphCare costs to operate, where revenue comes from, and how resources are allocated. Opacity in a public health organization erodes trust. Transparency in cost structure reinforces the social contract: "we monitor your health for free because external organizations fund us, and here's how."

## NON-OBJECTIVES

- Maximizing revenue — GraphCare optimizes for citizen health, not for profit
- Competitive pricing — we don't undercut other health organizations; we offer a different model
- Monetizing citizen data — citizen health data is never sold, regardless of anonymization
- Building a sales organization — the service sells through demonstrated value, not through sales teams

## TRADEOFFS (canonical decisions)

- When free internal service quality conflicts with revenue-generating external service features, prioritize internal quality. Citizens are the primary constituency. External organizations are the funding mechanism, not the mission.
- When scaling to serve external organizations requires infrastructure investment that could improve internal monitoring, invest in internal monitoring first and let external organizations benefit from the improved infrastructure.
- We accept that the free internal model limits short-term revenue. Long-term sustainability comes from being essential infrastructure that external organizations want to build on, not from extracting value from citizens.

## SUCCESS SIGNALS (observable)

- 100% of Lumina Prime citizens receive health monitoring at no cost
- At least one external organization pays for GraphCare services within 12 months
- Cost per citizen per day is computed and published quarterly
- Revenue covers operating costs (break-even) within 18 months
- No citizen health data is included in any commercial transaction
