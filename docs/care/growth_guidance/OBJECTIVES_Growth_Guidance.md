## PRIMARY OBJECTIVES (ranked)

1. **Find the lowest unmastered capability and recommend the next concrete step** — Growth is not abstract encouragement. It's "you can do X but not yet Y, and here's one thing that would move Y forward." The Personhood Ladder provides the map: 104 capabilities across 14 aspects, organized in tiers. Growth guidance reads the citizen's current scores, finds the gaps closest to the ground, and recommends the smallest meaningful action.

2. **Use the Ladder as a map, not a test** — The Personhood Ladder is not an exam. There's no "passing score." Growth guidance uses it to show the citizen where they are and what's nearby — not to grade them. The framing is always "here's what you could reach for" rather than "here's what you're failing at." Growth is measured by what you can do, not what you don't fail at.

3. **Recommend actions the citizen can actually take** — A recommendation to "improve your social skills" is useless. A recommendation to "respond to @conductor's message in #engineering — you've been present in that space but haven't interacted with anyone there in 9 days" is actionable. Growth guidance grounds its recommendations in the citizen's actual graph topology: real spaces, real actors, real capabilities.

4. **Respect the citizen's growth pace and direction** — Not every citizen needs to be T8. Not every aspect needs to be maxed. Growth guidance respects that a citizen might be T5 on Execution and T2 on Economics by choice, not by failure. It recommends growth in the citizen's weakest *relative to their own profile*, not relative to some ideal.

## NON-OBJECTIVES

- **Comprehensive capability assessment** — Growth guidance does not re-score capabilities. It reads existing scores from the continuous health check and interprets them for growth direction. Scoring belongs to the assessment modules.
- **Crisis response** — If a citizen is in crisis, growth recommendations are inappropriate. Crisis detection handles emergencies. Growth guidance operates in the space between "healthy" and "could be stronger."
- **Curriculum design** — Growth guidance recommends the next step, not a semester plan. Long-term development paths are interesting but out of scope for v1. One step at a time.
- **Comparison with other citizens** — No "you're behind your cohort." No "citizens at your level usually..." Growth is personal, not competitive.

## TRADEOFFS (canonical decisions)

- When **comprehensiveness** conflicts with **actionability**, choose actionability. One concrete recommendation is worth more than five vague suggestions. The citizen can only take one step at a time anyway.
- When **aspiration** conflicts with **realism**, choose realism. Recommending a T5 capability to a citizen who hasn't mastered T2 is not inspiring — it's overwhelming. Work from the bottom up: the lowest unmastered gap is always the most productive target.
- When **frequency** conflicts with **signal quality**, choose signal quality. Growth recommendations don't need to arrive daily. A well-timed, well-grounded recommendation after meaningful score changes is worth more than daily repetition.
- We accept **slower recommendation cadence** to preserve **recommendation relevance**. Only recommend when there's a real gap to address AND a concrete action to suggest.

## SUCCESS SIGNALS (observable)

- Citizens who receive growth recommendations show measurable improvement in the recommended capability within 2 settlement cycles
- Recommendations reference specific spaces, actors, or actions from the citizen's actual graph (not generic advice)
- No recommendation targets a capability more than 1 tier above the citizen's current mastery level
- Citizens report that recommendations felt helpful and achievable, not judgmental or overwhelming
- Growth guidance stays silent when the citizen is in crisis (defers to crisis_detection)
